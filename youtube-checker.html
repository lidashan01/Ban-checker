About the YouTube Ban Checker

Our tool provides a quick and reliable way to check the status of any YouTube channel. By using the official YouTube Data API, we can determine if a channel is active, has been terminated by YouTube for policy violations, or was deleted by its owner.

How does it work?

When you enter a channel's identifier (like its handle, custom URL, or ID), our tool queries the YouTube API. A successful response means the channel is active. If the API reports that the channel cannot be found, it is flagged as "Banned or Not Found." This typically means the channel was removed from the platform.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between a banned and a deleted channel?
From an API perspective, there's often no difference. Both result in the channel being inaccessible and will be flagged by our tool. A "banned" or "terminated" channel is removed by YouTube, while a "deleted" channel is removed by the creator.
Can this tool detect "shadowbans"?
"Shadowbanning" on YouTube (where a channel's reach is secretly reduced) is not an official status that the API can report. Our tool can only check for explicit bans or terminations where the channel is fully removed.
Is this service free?
Yes, our YouTube Ban Checker is completely free to use. We believe in providing simple and accessible tools for everyone.
