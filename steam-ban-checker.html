About The Steam Ban Checker

What is a Steam Ban?

Steam bans come in several forms, most notably VAC (Valve Anti-Cheat) Bans, Game Bans, and Community Bans. A VAC ban is permanent and prevents playing on secure servers for a specific game. A Game Ban is issued by a developer for non-cheating offenses, while a Community Ban restricts social features. Our Steam Ban Checker verifies all these statuses.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Enter Profile Info: Type or paste a user's SteamID64, custom URL, or full profile link into the box. You can check multiple accounts by entering each one on a new line.
  2. Start the Check: Click the "Check Status" button.
  3. View Results: The tool will display a card for each user, showing their ban status, including the type of ban (VAC, Game, etc.) and other details.

Why Do Steam Accounts Get Banned?

The most common reason for a permanent ban is cheating in a VAC-secured game. Other reasons include griefing, scamming, harassing other players, or violating Steam's Subscriber Agreement. To avoid a ban, always play fair, never use third-party cheating software, and respect other members of the community. This Steam Ban Checker helps you verify if an account has a clean record.

How to Appeal a Ban

According to Valve, VAC bans are permanent and non-negotiable and cannot be appealed. For Game Bans, you must contact the specific game's developer, as Valve does not handle these. If you believe your account was hijacked, you should first lock your account and then contact Steam Support to recover it. An incorrectly applied ban may be automatically removed, but this is rare.
