About Steam Ban Checker

What is a Steam Ban?

A Steam ban can come in several forms: VAC (Valve Anti-Cheat) bans for cheating in games, Game bans issued by developers, Community bans that restrict social features, and Trade bans that prevent trading items. VAC bans are permanent and prevent you from playing on secure servers for specific games. Our Steam Ban Checker helps you identify accounts with any of these restrictions.

How to Use This Tool

  1. Enter Steam IDs: Type or paste one or more Steam IDs or profile URLs into the input box above. Make sure each ID is on a new line.
  2. Start the Check: Click the "Check Accounts" button.
  3. View Results: The tool will process the list and sort the accounts into two categories: "Clean Accounts" and "Banned / Suspended / Not Found".

Why Do Steam Accounts Get Banned?

Steam accounts are typically banned for cheating (VAC bans), griefing, scamming, harassment, or violating Steam's terms of service. VAC bans are issued automatically when cheat software is detected. Game bans are issued by individual game developers for various offenses. To avoid bans, always play fair, never use cheating software, and follow community guidelines.

How to Appeal a Steam Ban

VAC bans are permanent and cannot be appealed - they are only issued when cheat software is definitively detected. Game bans must be appealed directly to the game developer, not Valve. If you believe your account was compromised, contact Steam Support immediately to secure your account.
