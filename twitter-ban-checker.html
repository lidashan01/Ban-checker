Twitter Bans, Locks, and Shadowbans Explained

Twitter's moderation system is complex, involving official penalties and more subtle forms of visibility filtering. Our Twitter Ban Checker helps you identify which, if any, of these apply to an account.

Official Penalties: Suspensions & Locks

  • Suspended Account: This is a direct penalty for violating Twitter's rules. Your account is made read-only, you cannot tweet, and your profile is hidden from public view. This requires a formal appeal to resolve.
  • Locked Account: This is usually a security measure. Twitter may lock your account if it detects suspicious login activity or suspects your account has been compromised. Unlocking it typically involves verifying your phone number or email.

The "Shadowban": Unofficial Visibility Filtering

A "shadowban" is not an official term from Twitter, but it describes several types of algorithmic visibility filtering that can happen without notification:

  • Search Ban: Your tweets will not appear in search results, even when people search for exact phrases you've used. This severely limits the reach of your content to non-followers.
  • Thread/Reply Deboosting: Your replies to other tweets are hidden behind a "Show more replies" button, sometimes even for your own followers. This makes it almost impossible to participate in public conversations.
  • Search Suggestion Ban: Your account will not appear in the search bar's auto-suggestions when people type your name.

How to Maintain a Healthy Twitter Account

1. Avoid Aggressive Following/Unfollowing

Rapidly following or unfollowing a large number of accounts in a short period is a classic bot behavior and a major red flag for Twitter's systems.

2. Be Careful with Automation

While using third-party apps to schedule tweets is fine, automating likes, follows, or direct messages is against the rules and can quickly lead to account suspension.

3. Engage Authentically

Don't post duplicate or near-duplicate tweets or replies. Participate in conversations genuinely. If your activity looks robotic or spammy, the algorithm may apply restrictions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Twitter Shadowban test safe?

Yes, our tool is 100% safe. We only use public Twitter data and do not require you to log in or grant any permissions to your account. Your privacy and account security are never at risk.

How do you test for a Twitter shadowban?

Our tool simulates how Twitter treats an account in public spaces. It checks if the account's tweets appear in search results, if the user appears in search suggestions, and if their replies are visible to logged-out users. If visibility is restricted in these areas, it indicates a type of shadowban.

Can a suspended Twitter account be recovered?

Yes, in many cases. If your account is suspended, you can file an appeal with Twitter Support. You'll need to explain why you believe the suspension was a mistake. The success of the appeal often depends on the severity of the violation and your account's history.

What's the difference between a suspended and a locked account?

A locked account is usually a temporary security measure because Twitter detected suspicious activity (like too many login attempts). You can typically unlock it by verifying your identity (e.g., via email or phone). A suspended account is a penalty for violating Twitter's rules, and it requires a formal appeal to be restored.

How long does a shadowban last on Twitter?

There is no official duration, but community reports suggest that most shadowbans last between 48 and 72 hours. During this time, it's often recommended to pause tweeting and interacting to allow the system's flags on your account to reset.
