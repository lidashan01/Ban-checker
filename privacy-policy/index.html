Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2025-07-10

At BanChecker ("we", "us", or "our"), we value your privacy and strive to keep our tools simple, transparent, and respectful of your data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using our website and tools, you agree to the practices described here.

We collect minimal information necessary to provide our services. When you use our tools, any account names or profile URLs you enter are processed to perform the requested checks. We do not create user accounts for our tools, and we do not knowingly collect sensitive personal data. Standard web server logs may include your IP address, browser type, referrer, and timestamps for security and diagnostics.

We use input data solely to perform the checks you request and to display the results to you. Operational metrics and aggregated usage patterns may be used to maintain, secure, and improve our services. We do not sell your personal information.

Cookies and Analytics

We may use cookies and similar technologies to remember preferences (such as dark mode) and to understand how our site is used. We may also use privacy-friendly analytics or third‑party analytics that provide aggregate statistics. You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect site functionality.

Data Retention

Input values submitted to our tools are processed in real time and typically not stored beyond what is necessary to complete the request. Diagnostic logs and security records may be retained for a limited period consistent with operational needs and legal obligations.

Your Choices and Rights

You may choose not to provide input data to our tools. You can clear cookies via your browser. If you have questions about access, correction, or deletion of any information related to you, please contact us and we will review your request in line with applicable laws.

Children’s Privacy

Our services are intended for a general audience and are not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us so we can delete it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law, or our services. When we do, we will revise the effective date above. Continued use of our site after changes means you accept the updated policy.
